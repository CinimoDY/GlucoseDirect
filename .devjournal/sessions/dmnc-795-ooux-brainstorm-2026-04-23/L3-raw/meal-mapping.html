Pattern 1 + explicit 1-tap favourite

Favourite tap = immediate log (keeps today's speed, by design). Long-press a favourite = open in staging plate for edit. Other methods → staging plate.

Pro: preserves the "log a pre-vetted favourite in one tap" speed people rely on. Con: one object (Meal) ends up with two commit paths (direct + staged).

Lean: C. Option A is architecturally purest but punishes a frequent workflow (regular meal-loggers with curated favourites, where today's one-tap is the whole point). Option B forces an axis ("entry method") that isn't really a meal-object facet. C acknowledges that Meal is a list-producing object (Pattern 1) but carves a designed exception for favourites — with long-press as the power-user escape to edit. The two paths are justified by "favourites are user-curated pre-vetted data" which already passed a review step when the favourite was created.
